Natural Attractions

Mount Kitanglad

A majestic mountain range and natural park that's home to diverse flora and fauna, including the Philippine eagle. It's a UNESCO-declared ASEAN Heritage Park and a favorite among mountaineers.

Rich Cultural Heritage

The Indigenous Tribes

Bukidnon is home to seven indigenous tribes: the Bukidnon, Higaonon, Talaandig, Manobo, Matigsalug, Tigwahanon, and Umayamnon. Each tribe has its own distinct traditions, languages, and cultural practices.

These indigenous communities maintain a deep spiritual connection with nature, practicing sustainable agriculture and preserving ancestral knowledge of herbal medicine and craftsmanship.

Traditional Architecture

The traditional houses of Bukidnon, called "balay," are built using indigenous materials like bamboo, rattan, and cogon grass. These structures are elevated on stilts, reflecting the people's adaptation to the mountainous terrain.

The design incorporates cultural symbols and is built without nails, using intricate weaving and knotting techniques passed down through generations.

Colorful Festivals

Kaamulan Festival

Bukidnon's most famous festival held from February to March, celebrating the culture and traditions of the seven indigenous tribes. The festival features street dancing, ethnic music, traditional games, and a grand tribal gathering.

Cultural Showcase Street Dancing Tribal Rituals

Pineapple Festival

Celebrated in June in the town of Don Carlos, this festival honors Bukidnon's thriving pineapple industry. The event features agricultural exhibits, cooking competitions, cultural shows, and the coronation of the Pineapple Festival Queen.

Delicious Cuisine

Binaki

A sweet corn cake wrapped in banana leaves, this Bukidnon delicacy is steamed to perfection, resulting in a moist and flavorful treat that's perfect for snacks or dessert.

Sweet Corn Cake

Sinuglaw

A fusion dish combining grilled pork (sinugba) and ceviche-style fish (kinilaw), representing the blending of land and sea flavors that's unique to Bukidnon's culinary tradition.

Pork & Fish Dish

Pinikpikan

A traditional chicken soup dish prepared with a unique method that involves lightly beating the chicken before cooking, believed to enhance the flavor and texture of the meat.

Chicken Soup
